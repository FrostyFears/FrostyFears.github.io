A Quick Background

I grew up in Southern California, the only child to my parents. My mom is Irish & Scottish primarily and my father was African American. In my early childhood I was sick due to complications to asthma and reoccurring pneumonia infections. My Parents during this time encouraged my enjoyment of reading which lead to a fascination with all sorts of things but primarily science. After my fathers passing I decided to head back to school to pursue higher education where I re-found my love of science particularly in the fields of bioinformatics and bioengineering.

About Me

A Little Bit on my Research

I attended Rio Hondo Community College in which I was a part of the Honors Transfer Program leading to my transfer to California State University, Long Beach where I earned my degree in Molecular Cell Biology and Physiology with a minor in Chemistry and Deans List. In the final year of my undergrad I began work with Dr. Perla Ayala in the field of bioengineering focusing my research on the development of mold specific tissue scaffolding via collagen hydrogels, and imbedding of progenitor cells for regeneration of the surrounding tissue in which it is imbedded. During this time my project Engineered Constructs with Aligned Topography for Muscle Growth was funded through the ORSP scholarship giving me the opportunity to present my work at the 2017 CSUPERB conference in San Jose, California. I also worked on another project, the integration of decellularized cardiac tissue proteins in a collagen support matrix for cell growth, which is currently being used in multiple student projects and presentations and was even part of a paper submitted in 2020 to the Proceedings of The National Conference On Undergraduate Research (NCUR). Through this work I have been co-author on six undergraduate poster presentations, examining various types of tissue constructed via our hydrogel, and the mechanical properties of the hydrogel; four of which were presented at national conferences; CSUPERB, BMES and ABRCMS.

In Spring of 2019 I was accepted into the Penn State PhD program for Bioinformatics & Genomics and awarded Braddock/Roberts Fellowship as well as the NIH Computation, Bioinformatics & Statistics (CBIOS) Training Fellowship. I continued my focus on research surrounding public health which led to my joining of Dr. Dajiang Liuâ€™s Lab, where my background in cardiac tissue design will aid in my research into rare causal variants of Coronary Artery Disease.

In the Summer of 2020 I was awarded the scholarship for the Summer Institute in Statistical Genetics (SISG) in Washington.

In Summer of 2021 I completed work on a consultation with Dr. Theodore DeMartini on his manuscript Pediatric Ventilator-associated Event Category Correlates Poorly with Clinically Meaningful Outcomes

The next year in February of 2022 I married my now wife Judith Rodriguez, whom I met in graduate school. We have gone on many adventures since (crossed the US in a car 4 times) and plan on so many more!

In summer of 2024 I was fortunate enough to be selected as the officiant for two of my close friends when they decided to get married. Me, my wife and these friends all met our first year in graduate school, in the same program! It is quite the story.

During the COVID pandemic I worked primarily on various consultations with Dr.s Chintan Gandhi, Ben White, Kara Dijoseph, Sarah Boyd and Jane Schubart. Of these two resulted in publications, one in 2022 and the other in 2023, which are featured under the publications tab on this site
